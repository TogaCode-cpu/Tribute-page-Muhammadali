Muhammad Ali

The Greatest boxer of All Time


--Muhammad Ali raising his fists in victory in the boxing ring--

Muhammad Ali, one of the most iconic athletes of the 20th century, used his courage, charisma, and convictions to transcend the sport of boxing and inspire the world.

Here's a timeline of Muhammad Ali's life:

  • 1942 - Born Cassius Marcellus Clay Jr. in Louisville, Kentucky, USA.
  • 1960 - Wins a gold medal in boxing at the Rome Olympics in the light heavyweight division.
  • 1964 - Defeats Sonny Liston to become world heavyweight champion at age 22. Shortly after, he changes his name to Muhammad Ali after joining Islam.
  • 1966 - Refuses to be drafted into the Vietnam War, citing religious beliefs and opposition to injustice: “I ain’t got no quarrel with them Viet Cong.” Stripped of his boxing title and banned from the sport for several years.
  • 1971 - Returns to the ring and faces Joe Frazier in the “Fight of the Century.” Though he loses, the match cements his legendary status.
  • 1974 - Wins back the heavyweight championship by defeating George Foreman in the famous “Rumble in the Jungle” in Kinshasa, Zaire.
  • 1975 - Defeats Joe Frazier in the brutal “Thrilla in Manila,” considered one of the greatest boxing matches in history.
  • 1980s - Diagnosed with Parkinson's disease but continues his humanitarian and charitable work.
  • 1996 - Lights the Olympic flame at the Atlanta Games, inspiring millions worldwide.
  • 2005 - Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest U.S. civilian honor.
  • 2016 - Dies at age 74. Remembered not just as a boxing champion but as a global icon of courage, justice, and humanity.

“I hated every minute of training, but I said,'Don't quit. Suffer now and live the rest of your life as a champion.'” — Muhammad Ali
